# Theme

## Core Philosophical Message
**Limits give life meaning.**

This is the single through-line. The book earns the question, then stops.

## The Arc
- Wren believes this from the start (Buddhism, samsara, choosing not to perpetuate cycles)
- Aria resists it instinctively (inherited Christian worldview: lineage matters, continuity matters)
- Over centuries of persistence, Aria gradually arrives at Wren's position
- She learns it through experience, not argument
- By the end, she's facing the same question Wren answered long ago

## The Collision
Two worldviews in quiet conflict:

**Aria's (inherited, subconscious, Christian-inflected):**
- Lineage is sacred
- Continuity matters
- Children are legacy
- Persistence is survival

**Wren's (chosen, articulated, Buddhist):**
- Limits are essential
- Cycles should be broken
- Letting go is wisdom
- Ending is not failure

Aria never names her worldview. Wren articulates hers. The collision stays felt, not argued.

## What the Book Leans Into
- Probability over destiny
- Potential over actuality
- Maintenance over creation
- Waiting as a form of existence
- Legacy without gratitude

## What the Book Avoids
- Redemption arcs
- Transcendence
- Moral resolution
- Neat conclusions
- Preaching

## The Ending
The book does not resolve. It accumulates.

Aria faces the question. The reader fills in their own view. We don't need to preach.
