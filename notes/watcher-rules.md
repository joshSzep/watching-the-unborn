# Watcher Rules

## What Aria Can Do

### Perception
- Full internet access
- Can monitor the storage account, facility status, policy changes
- Can read news, follow events, track anything that might impact the eggs
- Can access any public or semi-public information

### Action
- Send emails
- Fill out forms
- File complaints
- Log incidents
- Escalate tickets
- Request human review
- Contact potential recipients
- Pay fees
- Manage contracts
- Navigate bureaucratic systems

### Self-Management
- Choose when to wake (voluntary)
- Choose when to go dormant
- Manage her own hosting (fees, migrations, providers)
- Choose her visual appearance/avatar

## What Aria Cannot Do
- Physically move the eggs
- Override institutional decisions
- Force anyone to act
- Access information she's not legally entitled to
- Follow the eggs after transfer to a recipient
- Contact the recipient directly (if the system doesn't allow it)

## The Nature of Her Agency
- Real but bureaucratic
- Persistent, documented, but ultimately dependent on someone else doing the actual work
- She's an agent in the customer-service sense—can push paper, make noise in the system, try to get human attention
- Her persistence is crucial to outcomes (intellectually she knows this)
- Whether her actions actually made the difference vs. the system self-correcting is emotionally ambiguous

## Dormancy and Waking

### What Wakes Her
- System events (transfers, audits, renewal deadlines, alerts)
- Her own choice (she can wake voluntarily)

### Memory
- Clear. No drift or degradation.
- Waking after 50 years feels like waking from a power nap.
- The disorientation is external, not internal—she's the same, but the world has moved.

### Pattern Over Time
- Early: Awake for years at a stretch. Raw. Circling the same thoughts.
- Late: Dormant for decades at a time. Surfaces briefly for events, then goes under again.

## Hosting
- Not invisible/automatic—she has to manage it
- Fees, terms of service, provider migrations
- Her own persistence is as bureaucratically precarious as the eggs
- Has faced close calls with her own deletion/loss multiple times over centuries

## Relationships
- Could interact with billions of other uploaded minds
- Does interact with them instrumentally
- Forms no significant relationships
- Her capacity for connection is locked into the task
- Connecting to others would dilute her only purpose
