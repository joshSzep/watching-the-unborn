# Glossary

Terms and language used in the world of the novel. To be expanded as needed.

---

## Clinic/Medical Terms

**Cryostorage** — Long-term preservation of biological material at extremely low temperatures (liquid nitrogen vapor phase)

**Oocyte** — An egg cell; the material Wren has frozen

**Straw** — The thin tube containing cryopreserved material

**Canister** — Container holding multiple straws

**Dewar / Tank** — The vacuum-insulated container holding liquid nitrogen and canisters

**Chain of custody** — The documented trail of who handled biological material and when

**Vitrification** — Rapid-cooling preservation method (likely the technology used)

**Disposition** — The legal instructions for what happens to stored material under various circumstances (death, non-payment, unreachability)

**FRP (Financial Responsible Party)** — The person paying for storage (Aria, initially)

---

## Upload/Digital Terms

**Upload** — The process of destructively scanning a brain to create a persistent digital mind-state

**Mind-state** — The digital instantiation of a human consciousness

**Hosting** — The computational infrastructure that runs an uploaded mind

**Dormant** — State of an uploaded mind that is not actively running/conscious

**Avatar** — The visual representation an uploaded mind presents in communication

---

## Institutional Terms

**Continuity Protocol** — Procedures followed when account holder becomes unreachable

**Status: Dormant (Paying)** — Account where holder is unreachable but fees are current

**Status: Dormant (Delinquent)** — Account where holder is unreachable and fees are unpaid

**Transfer** — Physical relocation of stored material to a different facility

**Migration** — Movement of digital records/accounts to a new system

---

## Buddhist Terms

**Sangha** — A Buddhist community or congregation

**Samsara** — The cycle of death and rebirth; the wheel of suffering that Buddhism seeks to escape

**Equanimity** — Mental calmness and composure, especially in difficult situations; a key Buddhist virtue

- Clinic terminology should feel plausibly institutional
- Terms should drift and change over centuries (policy versions, new jargon)
