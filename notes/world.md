# World

## Setting Overview
- Deep future, but banal
- Upload technology is common by the 2120s
- Billions of uploaded minds exist
- Society is recognizably descended from ours, but has gone through collapses, recoveries, transformations
- The trajectory is not a smooth line—it's bumpy, sometimes ruptured, but still running

## Technology

### Upload Technology
- Common as an end-of-life option by 2120s
- The process: brain is dissected, not scanned. The body dies. The mind-state is created from the destruction.
- No duplication problem—the upload *is* the death
- Some people view uploaded minds as the same person continuing; others view it as death with a copy left behind
- The debate is never settled. People hold multiple, inconsistent views.
- Uploaded minds can: access the internet, communicate, manage accounts, exist indefinitely
- Uploaded minds must: manage their own hosting (fees, migrations, providers)

### Reproductive Technology
- Cryostorage of eggs/embryos exists from our present through the story's start
- Over 500 years, reproductive technology evolves dramatically
- By ~2600, Wren's eggs are archaic—stored in an obsolete format, requiring special handling
- The method no one uses anymore
- Finding someone willing to use them is part of the challenge

## The World Across Centuries
Aria only sees what might affect the eggs. The reader gets the shape of centuries through what she notices:

### Types of Events That Matter
- Climate events (threaten facilities)
- Political shifts (change storage law, jurisdiction)
- Infrastructure failures (power, cooling, data)
- Legal reclassifications (definitions of ownership, consent, personhood)
- Wars and displacement (evacuations, destruction)
- Corporate bankruptcies (assets in limbo)
- Energy crises (grid instability)
- Ideological movements (opposition to cryostorage, historical material)
- Data migrations (identifier schemes replaced, records orphaned)

### What We Don't See
- Anything that doesn't touch the eggs or Aria's hosting
- The broader sweep of history is implied, not narrated

## Los Angeles
- Starting location for the clinic
- Could plausibly exist in some form centuries later, even if unrecognizable
- California law has specific frameworks around reproductive material
- Legal regimes shift over time

## Uploaded Society
- Billions of uploaded minds
- Aria could interact with them
- She does interact instrumentally
- She forms no meaningful relationships
- The presence of community makes her solitude a choice, not an imposition
