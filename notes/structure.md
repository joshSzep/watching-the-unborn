# Structure

## Format
- **Chapters:** 36
- **Chapter names:** Descriptive titles (short phrases that name what the chapter holds without explaining it)
- **Average length:** ~3,000 words per chapter
- **Flexibility:** Some chapters can be short, some long
- **Total length:** ~108,000 words (full novel)

## Division

### Part One: Human Aria (Chapters 1-12)
- ~30 years of story time
- Third person, neutral angle
- Observing Aria and Wren from outside
- Beats to cover:
  - The clinic visit (eggs go into storage)
  - Time with Wren as adults (their dynamic, warmth and friction)
  - Wren finding Buddhism, meeting Jason
  - The four-way dynamic: Aria, Wren, Jason, and the eggs sitting in storage
  - Aria aging
  - The decision to upload
  - The upload itself (Wren and Jason present)

### Part Two: Uploaded Aria (Chapters 13-36)
- ~300 years of story time
- Locked inside Aria's perspective
- Beats to cover:
  - Early waking: raw, awake for years, watching eggs that don't change
  - 40 years with Wren still alive (video calls, the avatar, aging vs. not aging)
  - Wren's death
  - Reading the journals (immediate, all at once)
  - Staying awake, processing
  - Conversations with Jason
  - Jason's death
  - True solitude begins
  - Close calls with the eggs (multiple, across centuries)
  - Close calls with her own hosting
  - The search for a recipient
  - Increasing dormancy
  - The world changing around her
  - Technology making the eggs archaic
  - The stranger, the transfer
  - Losing access
  - The ending: awake, present, finally enough

## Time Handling
- Early: Clear dates, grounded
- Late: Blurry, impressionistic
- Time jumps encouraged
- Repetition across centuries is a feature

## Scene Types
- Document + scene pairing (artifact as entry point, then observation)
- Waiting room scenes (banal, procedural)
- Lab-adjacent scenes (hallway, door, badge boundaries)
- Video calls (Aria as avatar, watching Wren and Jason age)
- System interactions (forms, alerts, escalations)
- Processing/reflection (Aria alone with her thoughts)
- Conversations with Jason (the Buddhist perspective articulated)
- Close calls (transfer crises, mislabeling, policy threats)

## The Ending
- Aria awake, purposeless
- Thinking about the choice (persist vs. end)
- Thinking about Wren and Jason
- The question is visible, the answer is not
- The book stops mid-thought or at the edge of decision
