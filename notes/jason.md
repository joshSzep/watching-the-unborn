# Jason

## Basic Information
- **Profession:** Daytrader, then beekeeper (after a change of heart)
- **Died:** A few years after Wren (~2163-2165)

## Personality
- Someone who already made the turn—from accumulation to cycles, from screens to seasons.
- He knows what it looks like to let go of one life for another.
- Compassionate. Treats others with equanimity.
- Doesn't explain, defend, or fight. Seeks to help others suffer less and understand more.

## Background
- Was a daytrader. Had a change of heart. Became a beekeeper.
- Buddhist. Part of the sangha where he meets Wren.

## Relationship to Wren
- Meets Wren through the sangha.
- They share a worldview: limits matter, cycles matter, letting go matters.
- The relationship is what ends Wren's marriage to Bryce.
- They're together for decades.

## Relationship to Aria
- Warm. He becomes family to Aria despite being "the other man" who ended the marriage she approved of.
- Aria warms to him naturally because he makes Wren happy.
- After Wren dies, Jason becomes Aria's last human connection.

## The Secrets
- Jason knows everything Wren kept from Aria:
  - That Wren didn't want children
  - That Wren disapproved of the upload
  - That Wren disapproved of the avatar
- He keeps these secrets while Wren is alive.
- He keeps them after Wren dies—even while maintaining a warm relationship with Aria.

## The Conversation
- Eventually Aria learns Jason knew. They talk about it.
- Jason doesn't apologize or explain. He meets her with compassion.
- His role is not antagonist or explainer—he embodies what Wren believed.
- He tries to help Aria understand, to ease her toward the view Wren held.
- These conversations continue for the remainder of his life.

## Death
- Refuses to upload, like Wren.
- Dies naturally, a few years after Wren.
- His death marks the beginning of Aria's true solitude—centuries with no one who knew Wren.
