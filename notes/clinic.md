# The Clinic / Institutional System

## Starting Point
- Part of a large network from the beginning (explains stability over centuries)
- Located in Los Angeles, California
- California law has specific frameworks around reproductive material—matters when legal regimes shift

## Institutional Continuity
- The name changes through acquisitions over centuries
- Aria tracks the eggs through a series of corporate identities
- Each era has a different header on the forms
- The institution is a thread of paperwork, not a stable name

## How Cryostorage Works
- Oocytes (eggs) stored in liquid nitrogen vapor phase
- Straws, canisters, tanks, racks, positions
- Chain-of-custody documentation
- Two-person verification for handling
- Alarm systems
- Annual renewal cycles
- Consent forms with disposition elections

## What Can Go Wrong
Over centuries, the eggs face threats from:
- **Infrastructure failure:** Freezer alarms, power outages, equipment malfunction
- **Legal reclassification:** Changing definitions of ownership, consent, personhood
- **War/displacement:** Conflict forcing evacuations or destroying facilities
- **Corporate bankruptcy:** Companies failing, assets in limbo
- **Energy crisis:** Power grid instability
- **Ideological movements:** Opposition to cryostorage, historical material, etc.
- **Data migration:** Identifier schemes replaced, records partially orphaned

## Physical Transfers
- The eggs move many times over centuries
- Each transfer is a moment of tension—chain-of-custody risk, mislabeling, transit failure
- These are dramatic events in the narrative
- Aria can flag discrepancies, file alerts, but can't physically intervene

## Evolution of the Technology
- By the end (300+ years later), reproductive technology has evolved
- Wren's eggs become archaic—a quaint, old-fashioned container
- Obsolete format requiring special handling
- The method no one uses anymore
- Finding someone willing to use them becomes part of the difficulty

## The Final Transfer
- A stranger eventually uses the eggs
- Transaction entirely mediated through the system
- Forms, approvals, transfers—no direct contact between Aria and recipient
- The stranger remains anonymous
- After transfer, Aria loses all legal connection to follow the outcome
