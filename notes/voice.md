# Voice

## The POV Shift

### Pre-Upload (Chapters 1-12)
- Third person
- Neutral angle—observing Aria and Wren from outside
- Creates distance, matches clinical settings
- Saves Aria's interiority for later

### Post-Upload (Chapters 13-36)
- Locked inside Aria's perspective for the rest of the book
- Centuries from one point of view
- We experience only what she experiences

## Aria's Voice

### Qualities
- Emotion leaks through—she's still a human mind
- Restraint in the prose isn't because she's machine-like
- It's because emotion is real but suppressed
- Feeling comes through word choice, repetition, what she notices

### Evolution Over Centuries
- Early: Raw, circling, present
- Late: Wiser, less controlling, sparser
- The change is gradual—only visible when comparing early and late sections
- No single turning point

## Prose Style Guidelines

### Preferred
- Clinical language
- Precision beats beauty
- Show feelings through repetition, omission, pattern
- Calm prose even when implications are not

### To Avoid
- Poetic excess
- Sentimentality
- Hope as a theme
- Explaining feelings directly

### The Rule
If something feels profound, underwrite it.

## Documents and Artifacts
- Can serve as entry points to scenes
- A header, a checkbox, a discrepancy—then shift to observation
- Not the whole texture, just one surface
- Meaning leaks through what the system records badly: redactions, missing fields, policy version bumps

## Human Voices
- Can appear as "captured" material: voicemail transcripts, complaint calls, clinician note fields, forwarded emails
- Aria rereads these across decades because they're the only texture that persists

## Time and Blur
- Early chapters: clearer dates, grounded time
- Later chapters: time becomes impressionistic, blurry
- Aria may stop tracking years precisely
- The reader should feel the blur
