# Outline

36 chapters. Descriptive titles. ~3,000 words average.

---

## Part One: Human Aria (Chapters 1-12)
*Third person, neutral angle. ~30 years. Observing from outside.*

### Chapter 1: The Waiting Room
The clinic visit. Aria and Wren in the waiting room. Forms. The hallway Aria can't enter. The eggs go into storage.

### Chapter 2: After the Divorce
Recent past. Wren's marriage to Bryce ending. Wren finding Buddhism. Aria processing the loss of the future she expected.

### Chapter 3: Jason
Wren introduces Jason to Aria. The beekeeper. The man who replaced the cop. Aria's initial reservation, then warming.

### Chapter 4: The Dinner
A gathering: Aria, Wren, Jason. The dynamic between them. Warmth through friction. What's said and unsaid.

### Chapter 5: Renewal
Years later. An annual storage renewal notice. Aria pays. Wren doesn't mention the eggs. The routine continues.

### Chapter 6: The Question
Aria asks Wren about grandchildren. Wren deflects. The conversation that goes nowhere. What Aria doesn't see.

### Chapter 7: The Practice
Wren and Jason's Buddhist life. Aria observing from outside. The sangha. A world she doesn't understand but accepts.

### Chapter 8: Aria Aging
Years passing. Aria's body beginning to slow. Her work as an actuary. Planning for her own contingencies.

### Chapter 9: The Offer
Upload technology. Aria researching. The decision forming. A way to stay.

### Chapter 10: The Conversation
Aria tells Wren she's going to upload. "To be with you longer." Wren's reaction—what she says, what she doesn't.

### Chapter 11: The Facility
The upload facility. Different from the fertility clinic, but similarly institutional. Paperwork. Consent forms. Preparation.

### Chapter 12: The Procedure
The upload. Wren and Jason present. Aria's last moment in her body. The transition.

---

## Part Two: Uploaded Aria (Chapters 13-36)
*Inside Aria's perspective. ~500 years.*

### Chapter 13: Waking
First moments as an uploaded mind. Orientation. Choosing the avatar. The strangeness of perception without body.

### Chapter 14: The First Call
Video call with Wren and Jason. Aria at 25, Wren at 60. The uncanny. What's different, what's the same.

### Chapter 15: The Account
Aria accesses the storage account for the first time as an uploaded mind. The eggs. The dashboard. The long watch begins.

### Chapter 16: Years Awake
Time passing. Aria staying awake, watching. Thinking. The eggs that don't change. The world that does.

### Chapter 17: Wren at Seventy
A decade later. Wren aging. The calls continue. The avatar stays young. The silence around what Wren really thinks.

### Chapter 18: The Argument
Aria tries to convince Wren to upload. Wren refuses. The Buddhist reasoning. Limits give life meaning. The conversation that matters.

### Chapter 19: Jason's Bees
A call with Jason. The apiary. His philosophy. A different way of seeing time and cycles.

### Chapter 20: The First Transfer
A close call. The clinic is acquired. The eggs must move. Chain-of-custody anxiety. Aria files forms, sends emails, waits.

### Chapter 21: Wren at Ninety
Decades later. Wren very old. Fewer calls. The weight of watching someone approach death while you don't.

### Chapter 22: The Death
Wren dies. Natural. Expected. Final. Aria receives the notification. The inheritance.

### Chapter 23: The Journals
Aria reads Wren's private notes. All at once. The truth: no children wanted, disapproval of eggs, upload, avatar. Everything.

### Chapter 24: Processing
Aria stays awake. Time is squishy. The weight of what she now knows. The reframing: the eggs are what remains of Wren.

### Chapter 25: The Conversation with Jason
Aria confronts Jason. He knew. He kept quiet. His response: compassion, equanimity, trying to help her understand.

### Chapter 26: Jason's Way
More conversations with Jason. The Buddhist perspective. Suffering. Letting go. The view Aria is beginning to grasp.

### Chapter 27: Jason's Death
Jason dies. Refuses upload. The last person who knew Wren is gone. True solitude begins.

### Chapter 28: The Long Watch
Time passing. Dormancy increasing. Waking for events. The clinic changing hands. The eggs persisting.

### Chapter 29: The Close Call
A crisis. Infrastructure failure, or policy threat, or something else. Aria's persistence makes the difference—or does it?

### Chapter 30: The World Changed
Waking after a long dormancy. The world unrecognizable. New systems, new language, new institutions. The eggs still there.

### Chapter 31: Archaic
The eggs are now old technology. Obsolete format. Finding someone willing to use them is the new challenge.

### Chapter 32: The Search
Aria actively searching for a recipient. Filing forms, making contacts, navigating systems that keep changing.

### Chapter 33: Another Crisis
Another close call. Her own hosting this time, or the eggs, or both. The precariousness of persistence.

### Chapter 34: The Match
A stranger looking for something. Aria's centuries of keeping the eggs visible. A convergence. The transfer begins.

### Chapter 35: The Transfer
Paperwork. Approvals. The eggs leave Aria's custody. No direct contact with the stranger. A closed file.

### Chapter 36: The Question
Aria awake. Purposeless. Thinking about Wren, about Jason, about the choice they made. The question she's now facing. The book ends.

## Notes

- Chapter titles are placeholders—refine during drafting
- Time between chapters varies wildly (days to centuries)
- Part Two should feel compressed despite longer timespan
- Close calls can be moved/added as needed
- The ending should stop at the edge of decision, not after
